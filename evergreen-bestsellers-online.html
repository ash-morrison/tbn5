When Perennial Bestsellers Become “Evergreen” Online

Algorithms extend life cycles long after frontlist windows close.

Rafael CortesFebruary 18, 2024Seattle, WA

Online, breadth is cheap and attention is the scarce good. That’s why metadata and cover refreshes have become the midwife of evergreen backlist. ‘We audit keywords and categories every quarter,’ said Leo Martins, director of e‑com at Beacon House. ‘A tiny metadata tweak can unlock a new audience.’

Algorithms favor steady engagement—saves, samples, reviews—which publishers now court with serialized extras, reading guides, and cross‑promos. ‘We’re building ecosystems around certain properties,’ Martins said. ‘Think of it as soft serialization after publication.’

International markets compound the effect. A series that catches in one language can echo across territories, particularly in audio. ‘One breakout narrator in Spanish or German can pull a whole catalog forward,’ said Sofia Reinhardt, head of global audio at Axion.

The risk is complacency: mistaking the long tail for a set‑and‑forget channel. ‘Evergreen doesn’t mean automatic,’ Reinhardt warned. ‘It’s maintenance. The best teams garden their backlist.’
